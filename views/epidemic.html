Epidemiology and pandemics

1. What is a pandemic?

A pandemic (from Greek , pan, "all" and δῆμος, demo, "local" 'crowd') is an epidemic of an infectious disease that has spread over a large area , such as multiple continents or worldwide, affecting significant numbers of individuals. A widespread disease with a stable population of infected individuals is not a pandemic.

2. Pandemic flu

. The 1918 influenza pandemic, which lasted until 1919, was a type 5 influenza pandemic caused by an unusually deadly and severe influenza A virus. Victims are usually healthy young adults, in contrast to most influenza outbreaks, which mainly affect adolescent, elderly, or frail patients.

Older estimates say it killed 40–50 million people, [175] while more recent research suggests that it may have killed around 100 million, or 5% of the world's population in 1918.

4. Coronavirus

A related coronavirus, severe acute respiratory syndrome coronavirus 2 (SARS-Cov-2), believed to have originated in bats, emerged in Wuhan, China in November 2019 and spread spread rapidly around the world. Infection with the virus caused the COVID-19 pandemic that began in 2020.
