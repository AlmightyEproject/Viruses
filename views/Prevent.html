Prevention and treatment

Because viruses use important metabolic pathways in host cells to replicate, they are difficult to eliminate without the use of drugs that are toxic to the host cell in general. The most effective medical approaches to viral diseases are vaccination to provide immunity to the infection and antiviral drugs that selectively interfere with replication. of the virus.

1.Vaccines

Vaccination is a cheap and effective way to prevent viral infections. Vaccines have been used to prevent viral infections long before the actual virus was discovered. Their use has significantly reduced the morbidity (illness) and mortality (death) associated with viral infections such as polio, measles, mumps, and rubella. Live vaccines can be dangerous when given to people with weakened immunity (described as immunocompromised), because in these people the weakened virus can cause the initial illness. 3.cAntiviral drugs

Antiviral drugs are often nucleoside analogues (pseudo-DNA building blocks), which viruses mistakenly incorporate into their genomes during replication. [224] The life cycle of the virus is then stopped because the newly synthesized DNA is inactive. This is because these analogs lack hydroxyl groups, which, along with phosphorus atoms, bind together to form the strong "backbone" of the DNA molecule.

This is called DNA strand termination. [225] Examples of nucleoside analogues are aciclovir for Herpes simplex virus infection and lamivudine for HIV and hepatitis B virus infection. Aciclovir is one of the oldest and commonly prescribed antiretroviral drugs. most frequent.
