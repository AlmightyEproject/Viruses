Epidemiology

1. What is Epidemiology?

Epidemiology is the study and analysis of the distribution, patterns, and determinants of health and disease in a defined population. Viral epidemiology is a branch of the medical science concerned related to the transmission and control of viral infections in humans. Transmission of the virus can be vertical, meaning from mother to child, or horizontal, meaning from person to person.

2.Vertical Transmission

Vertical symbiotic transmission is the symbiotic transfer of microorganisms from direct parents to offspring. Many metazoan species carry symbiotic bacteria that have a reciprocal, symbiotic, or parasitic role. A parasite acquired by the host through horizontal, vertical, or mixed transmission.

3.Horizontal Transmission

Horizontal transmission is the most common mode of transmission of viruses in populations. [164] Horizontal transmission can occur when bodily fluids are exchanged during sexual activity, by exchange of saliva, or when contaminated food or water is ingested. It can also occur by inhalation of aerosols containing the virus or by an insect vector, such as when an infected mosquito penetrates the skin of a host. [164] Most viruses are restricted to only one or two of these mechanisms and they are referred to as "respiratory viruses" or "intestinal viruses", etc. Rate or rate of transmission Viral infection depends on factors including population density, the number of susceptible individuals, (i.e. those without immunity), [165] quality of health care, and weather.

Solution

The control measures used are based on knowledge of how the virus is transmitted. It is important to find the source, or sources of the outbreak, and identify the virus. Once the virus has been identified, the chain of transmission can sometimes be broken by vaccines. When a vaccine is not available, cleaning and disinfection can be effective. Normally, people who are infected are isolated from the rest of the community, and those who have been exposed to the virus are placed in quarantine.
